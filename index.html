Speaker Credibility

Thesis

The origin of graphic design along with what it has come to today makes it a perfect major for creative minded students. Graphic design has grown into a field where artistic passions can be put into a variety of jobs to help various kinds of companies and businesses through visual communication.

Preview of Main Points

Today, I will touch on the history of graphic design, the unique program it has here at KState, and future opportunities for graphic design students to give you all a better understanding of the major itself.

Body

Main Point

But first, You all might be wondering, what exactly is graphic design. Philip Meggs describes graphic design as, “the art and profession of selecting and arranging visual elements—such as typography, images, symbols, and colours—to convey a message to an audience.”

Subpoint 1

Professor, Philip Meggs, wrote an article on Britannica.com on November 7th, 2017, stating that Graphic design officially came about in the early 1920s when advertising agencies started hiring actual artists to create such forms of visual communication.

Subpoint 2

Before this, the marketing teams with little artistic schooling would try to design things like packaging and other forms of visual communication. These graphic designers really changed advertising and other forms of communication by adding professional artistic taste to companies brands.

As technology rapidly advanced, so did the field of graphic designers.

As said on Britanica.com, “Graphic designers began creatively and strategically designing magazine pages, book jackets, posters, compact-disc covers, postage stamps, packaging, trademarks, signs, advertisements, kinetic titles for television programs and motion pictures, and Websites.”

Transition

The history and origins of graphic design play into the necessary qualifications for someone to be a successful designer today.

Mainpoint

The graphic design program here at KState is very competitive and unique to other programs. Students striving to become graphic designers must inhabit many specific strengths and interests, just like any other major, in order to get accepted into the program.

Subpoint 1

Graphic design requires more right brained, or creative minded people. They must be able to create new ideas by still following the fundamentals of the elements of design which one learns in entry level art classes.

Subpoint 2

Designers must also be good at working with others. Therefore, Kstate recommends these students to minor in journalism and communications to prepare them for their careers.

Subpoint 3

Graphic design majors here at kstate will have their freshman and sophomore years consisting of classes on the tract of a BFA student, a bachelors in fine art. These classes are entry level art, design, and drawing classes that teach students to master the fundamentals of design.

Subpoint 4

Throughout these classes, students will be building a portfolio to present in a very formal interview setting in front of a panel of administrators in the art program in order to try to be accepted into the official graphic design program for their junior and senior year.

Subpoint 5

The overview of the graphic design program on the kansas state website states, “Through a mixture of training and experience in visual communications design, the program develops students’ creative ingenuity and problem-solving capabilities, while expanding technical and visual skill sets across industry-relevant media, materials, and environments. Students strive to develop strong, individualized portfolios that demonstrate risk-taking and inventiveness in form and concept.” This means that the competitive graphic design program at kstate forms designers that are also well rounded artists that will be prepared for what comes next after graduation.

Subpoint 6

Kristen Nowlin, an instructor in the art department here at Kansas State mentioned in an interview with me on September 18th, 2018, “The program is not easy to get into, the students must present their portfolios in ways that stick out above the rest. Internships are not required but they help students look ready for the next step. This program, like any other here at kstate, gets students ready for what comes next but the students must be ready and dedicated to get there”

Subpoint 7

Something that really strengthens Kstate’s program is their internship connections. They ensure that all students will have internship experience before graduation to strengthen their resumes and experience. As well as offering an incredible study abroad opportunity in Italy for students to take classes and experience a different culture.

Transition

Kansas State’s tough, competitive, and accredited program prepares students to be more than ready to take on the future. They go above the basics and give students many great opportunities to stand out and take on the next step.

Mainpoint

If you were unfamiliar with graphic design before this morning, you many not be aware of the job opportunities available with this degree.

Subpoint 1

Almost all big companies have their own graphic designers that work hand in hand with their advertising teams to create things like magazine ads, billboards, and more.

Subpoint 2

There are also design firms and marketing agencies that hire graphic designers for different clients of businesses big and small. Blue Fountain Media is the number one design firm in New York, which is a huge goal of mine. Gabriel Shaoolian, a talented visual designer told awards.com, “They provide 360 degree solutions from strategy and design to development and digital marketing services, with ROI as their factor for success. Their clients range from world-renown names like P&G, AT&T, NFL, and Canon, to emerging companies looking to grow their online presence.

Subpoint 3

Fremont College website lists other careers like web designers, logo designers, photo editors and multimedia designers as top careers with this degree. There are so many different areas of graphic design and because of this, there are many job opportunities out there that allow designers to excel in their specific area of interest.

Conclusion

In conclusion, graphic design has grown into a field where creative people can put their passions into a variety of jobs to help various kinds of companies through visual communication. The competitive graphic design program here at kstate is preparing students to do just that by mastering the fundamentals of art and design and preparing them for their future successes.
